Congestal

Description:
Congestal tablets are used for Common cold, Flu, Cold, Fever, Itchy throat/skin, Sinus congestion and pressure, allergies, temporary relief of cough caused by common cold, flu, rhinitis or other conditions. 
Congestal tablet contains Chlorpheniramine, Dextromethorphan, Paracetamol and Pseudoephedrine as active ingredients.

How does it work?
Congestal tablet works by blocking H1-receptor sites on tissues; decreasing activity in the part of the brain that causes coughing; increasing the pain threshold and increases the blood flow across the skin, heat loss and sweating; causing narrowing of the blood vessels and reduces swelling in the nasal passages.

Uses of Congestal Tablet:
Congestal tablet is used for the treatment, control, prevention, & improvement of the following diseases, conditions and symptoms:
* Common cold
* Flu
* Cold
* Fever
* Itchy throat/skin
* Sinus congestion and pressure
* Allergies
* Rhinitis
* Temporarily relief of cough caused by common cold, flu, or other conditions

Side-effects:
The following is a list of possible side-effects that may occur from all constituting ingredients of Congestal tablet. Consult your doctor if you observe any of the following side-effects, especially if they do not go away.
* Breathing problems
* Stomach pain
* Ringing in ears
* Blurred vision
* Painful urination
* Dry mouth
* Urge to vomit
* Allergic reactions
* Liver damage
If you notice other side-effects not listed above, contact your doctor for medical advice. You may also report side-effects to your local food and drug administration authority.

Precautions:
Before using Congestal tablet, inform your doctor about your current list of medications, over the counter products (e.g. vitamins, herbal supplements, etc.), allergies, pre-existing diseases, and current health conditions (e.g. pregnancy, upcoming surgery, etc.). Some health conditions may make you more susceptible to the side-effects of the drug. Take as directed by your doctor or follow the direction printed on the product insert. Dosage is based on your condition. Tell your doctor if your condition persists or worsens. Important counselling points are listed below.
* Ask a doctor before use if you have any breathing problems, glaucoma, or difficulty urinating
* Asthma
* Avoid alcohol and other sedatives while on this medication
* Avoid driving or operating heavy machinery until you know how this medication affects you
* Avoid driving or operating machinery until you know how this drug will affect you
* Avoid taking this drug too close to bedtime
* Avoid using it if allergic to paracetamol
* Chronic bronchitis
* Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease
* Cough with a large amount of phlegm

Interactions with Congestal tablet:
If you use other drugs or over the counter products at the same time, the effects of Congestal tablet may change. This may increase your risk for side-effects or cause your drug not to work properly. Tell your doctor about all the drugs, vitamins, and herbal supplements you are using, so that you doctor can help you prevent or manage drug interactions. Congestal tablet may interact with the following drugs and products:
* Alcohol
* Amiodarone
* Antipsychotics
* Cardiac glycosides
* Cough and cold medicines
* Haloperidol
* Interfere with certain laboratory tests
* Juxtapid mipomersen
* Ketoconazole
* Leflunomide

When not to use Congestal tablet:
Hypersensitivity to Congestal tablet is a contraindication. In addition, Congestal tablet should not be used if you have the following conditions:
* Allergic reactions
* Concomitant use with a monoamine oxidase inhibitor
* Hepatic impairment
* Hypersensitivity
* Hypertension
* Phaeochromocytoma
* Use within 2 weeks of discontinuing a monoamine oxidase inhibitor

Composition and Active Ingredients:

* Paracetamol
* Pseudoephedrine

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs):
Q: Is Congestal tablet safe to use when pregnant?
A. No, due to Chlorpheniramine, Dextromethorphan, Paracetamol, Pseudoephedrine it is unsafe to take congestal tablet during pregnancy.
Q. Is Congestal tablet safe while breastfeeding?
A. No, due to Chlorpheniramine, Dextromethorphan, Paracetamol, Pseudoephedrine it is unsafe to take congestal tablet while breastfeeding and please discuss the risks with your doctor..
Q. Can Congestal tablet be used for common cold and flu?
A. Yes, common cold and flu are among the most common reported uses for Congestal tablet. Please do not use Congestal tablet for common cold and flu without consulting first with your doctor. 
Q. How long do I need to use a Congestal tablet before I see improvement of my conditions?
A. Many people have reported the same day and within 6 hours as the most common time it takes before they see improvements in their conditions. These times may not be reflective of what you may experience or how you should use this medicine. Please consult with your doctor to check how long you need to use a Congestal tablet. 
Q. At what frequency do I need to use a Congestal tablet?
A. Many people have reported twice a day and thrice a day as the most common frequency of using Congestal tablet. Please follow your doctor's advice on how often you need to Congestal tablet.
Q. Should I use Congestal tablet empty stomach, before food or after food?
A. Many people have most commonly reported using Congestal tablet after food. However, this may not be reflective of how you should use this medicine. Please follow your doctor's advice on how you should use this medicine.
Q. Is it safe to drive or operate heavy machinery when using this product?
If you experience drowsiness, dizziness, hypotension or a headache as side-effects when using Congestal tablet medicine then it may not be safe to drive a vehicle or operate heavy machinery. One should not drive a vehicle if using the medicine makes you drowsy, dizzy or lowers your blood-pressure extensively. Pharmacists also advise patients not to drink alcohol with medicines as alcohol intensifies drowsiness side-effects. Please check for these effects on your body when using a Congestal tablet. Always consult with your doctor for recommendations specific to your body and health conditions.
Q. Is this medicine or product addictive or habit forming?
A. Most medicines don't come with a potential for addiction or abuse. Usually, the government categorises medicines that can be addictive as controlled substances. Please consult the product package to make sure that the medicine does not belong to such special categorizations of medicines. Lastly, do not self-medicate and increase your body's dependence to medicines without the advice of a doctor.
Q. Can I stop using this product immediately or do I have to slowly wean off the use?
A. Some medicines need to be tapered or cannot be stopped immediately because of rebound effects. Please consult with your doctor for recommendations specific to your body, health and other medications that you may be using.

Missing a dose:
In case you miss a dose, use it as soon as you notice. If it is close to the time of your next dose, skip the missed dose and resume your dosing schedule. Do not use an extra dose to make up for a missed dose. If you are regularly missing doses, consider setting an alarm or asking a family member to remind you. Please consult your doctor to discuss changes in your dosing schedule or a new schedule to make up for missed doses, if you have missed too many doses recently.

Overdosage of Congestal tablet
* Do not use more than prescribed dose. Taking more medication will not improve your symptoms; rather they may cause poisoning or serious side-effects. If you suspect that you or anyone else who may have overdosed on a Congestal tablet, please go to the emergency department of the closest hospital or nursing home. Bring a medicine box, container, or label with you to help doctors with necessary information.
* Do not give your medicines to other people even if you know that they have the same condition or it seems that they may have similar conditions. This may lead to overdose.
* Please consult your physician or pharmacist or product package for more information.

Storage of Congestal tablet:
* Store medicines at room temperature, away from heat and direct light. Do not freeze medicines unless required by package insert. Keep medicines away from children and pets.
* Do not flush medications down the toilet or pour them into drainage unless instructed to do so. Medication discarded in this manner may contaminate the environment. Please consult your pharmacist or doctor for more details on how to safely discard Congestal tablets.

Expired Congestal tablet:
Taking a single dose of expired Congestal tablet is unlikely to produce an adverse event. However, please discuss with your primary health provider or pharmacist for proper advice or if you feel unwell or sick. Expired drugs may become ineffective in treating your prescribed conditions. To be on the safe side, it is important not to use expired drugs. If you have a chronic illness that requires taking medicine constantly such as heart condition, seizures, and life-threatening allergies, you are much safer keeping in touch with your primary health care provider so that you can have a fresh supply of unexpired medications.
