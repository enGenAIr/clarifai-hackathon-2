Atoreza

Detailed Name: Atoreza 10/10 MG 28 Film Coated Tablets

Description:
Atoreza Tablet is used for High cholesterol level, Cardiovascular disease, Primary hyperlipidemia, High cholesterol, Dyslipidemia, Prevention of atherosclerotic stroke, Prevention of heart attack, Homozygous sitosterolemia and other conditions. 

How does Atoreza work?
Atoreza Tablet works by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase and reduces triglycerides levels in the blood; inhibiting the absorption of cholesterol in the intestine;

Uses:
Atoreza Tablet is used for the treatment, control, prevention, & improvement of the following diseases, conditions and symptoms:
* High cholesterol level
* Cardiovascular disease
* Primary hyperlipidemia
* High cholesterol
* Dyslipidemia
* Prevention of atherosclerotic stroke
* Prevention of heart attack
* Homozygous sitosterolemia

Side-effects:
The following is a list of possible side-effects that may occur from all constituting ingredients of Atoreza Tablet.These side-effects are possible, but do not always occur. Some of the side-effects may be rare but serious. Consult your doctor if you observe any of the following side-effects, especially if they do not go away.
* Muscle pain
* Type 2 diabetes mellitus
* Myopathy
* Joint pain
* Diarrhea
* Upset stomach
* Nausea
* Fatigue
* Influenza
* Sinusitis
* Upper respiratory tract infection
* Musculoskeletal and connective tissue disorders
* Arthralgia
* Pain in extremity
If you notice other side-effects not listed above, contact your doctor for medical advice. You may also report side-effects to your local food and drug administration authority.

Precautions:
Before using Atoreza Tablet, inform your doctor about your current list of medications, over the counter products (e.g. vitamins, herbal supplements, etc.), allergies, pre-existing diseases, and current health conditions (e.g. pregnancy, upcoming surgery, etc.). Some health conditions may make you more susceptible to the side-effects of the drug. Take as directed by your doctor or follow the direction printed on the product insert. Dosage is based on your condition. Tell your doctor if your condition persists or worsens. Important counselling points are listed below.
* Alcoholism
* Avoid excess alcohol while on atorvastatin
* Avoid grapefruit and grapefruit juice
* Avoid intake of excess fat
* Be careful if you have impairment of fertility
* Consult a doctor before taking medications such as cyclosporine, warfarin, phenprocoumon, acenocoumarol, cholestyramine, or fibrates
* Consult a doctor if you have hepatic insufficiency, carcinogenesis or liver disease
* Consult your doctor if you develop muscle pains, yellowing of skin/eyes, or an allergic reaction
* Do not drive or operate machinery or engage in activities requiring mental alertness and coordination
* Do not take atoreza if pregnant

Interactions with Atoreza Tablet:
If you use other drugs or over the counter products at the same time, the effects of Atoreza Tablet may change. This may increase your risk for side-effects or cause your drug not to work properly. Tell your doctor about all the drugs, vitamins, and herbal supplements you are using, so that your doctor can help you prevent or manage drug interactions. Atoreza Tablet may interact with the following drugs and products:
* Acetaminophen
* Amlodipine
* Antacids
* Aprepitant
* Aspirin
* Atorvastatin
* Azole antifungals
* Celecoxib
* Clopidogrel
* Colchicine

When not to use Atoreza Tablet:
Hypersensitivity to Atoreza Tablet is a contraindication. In addition, Atoreza Tablet should not be used if you have the following conditions:
* Active liver disease
* Hypersensitivity
* Lactation
* Moderate to severe liver disease
* Pregnancy
* Unexplained serum transaminase elevation

Composition and Active Ingredients
Atoreza Tablet is composed of the following active ingredients (salts)
* Atorvastatin
* Ezetimibe

Please note that this medicine may be available in various strengths for each active ingredient listed above.

Frequently asked Questions
Q. Is Atoreza Tablet safe to use when pregnant?
A. No, due to Atorvastatin and Ezetimibe present in this medicine.
Q. Is Atoreza Tablet safe while breastfeeding?
A. No, due to Atorvastatin and Ezetimibe present in this medicine.
Q. Can Atoreza Tablet be used for high cholesterol level and cardiovascular disease?
A. Yes, high cholesterol level and cardiovascular disease are among the most common reported uses for Atoreza Tablet. Please do not use Atoreza Tablet for high cholesterol level and cardiovascular disease without consulting first with your doctor. 
Q. Should I use an Atoreza Tablet empty stomach, before food or after food?
A. Many people have most commonly reported using Atoreza Tablet after food. However, this may not be reflective of how you should use this medicine. Please follow your doctor's advice on how you should use this medicine. 
Q. Is it safe to drive or operate heavy machinery when using this product?
A. If you experience drowsiness, dizziness, hypotension or a headache as side-effects when using Atoreza Tablet medicine then it may not be safe to drive a vehicle or operate heavy machinery. One should not drive a vehicle if using the medicine makes you drowsy, dizzy or lowers your blood-pressure extensively. Pharmacists also advise patients not to drink alcohol with medicines as alcohol intensifies drowsiness side-effects. Please check for these effects on your body when using Atoreza Tablet. Always consult with your doctor for recommendations specific to your body and health conditions.
Q. Is this medicine or product addictive or habit forming?
A. Most medicines don't come with a potential for addiction or abuse. Usually, the government categorizes medicines that can be addictive as controlled substances. Please consult the product package to make sure that the medicine does not belong to such special categorizations of medicines. Lastly, do not self-medicate and increase your body's dependence to medicines without the advice of a doctor.
Q. Can I stop using this product immediately or do I have to slowly wean off the use?
A. Some medicines need to be tapered or cannot be stopped immediately because of rebound effects. Please consult with your doctor for recommendations specific to your body, health and other medications that you may be using.

Missing a dose:
In case you miss a dose, use it as soon as you notice. If it is close to the time of your next dose, skip the missed dose and resume your dosing schedule. Do not use extra dose to make up for a missed dose. If you are regularly missing doses, consider setting an alarm or asking a family member to remind you. Please consult your doctor to discuss changes in your dosing schedule or a new schedule to make up for missed doses, if you have missed too many doses recently.

Overdosage of Atoreza Tablet:
* Do not use more than prescribed dose. Taking more medication will not improve your symptoms; rather they may cause poisoning or serious side-effects. If you suspect that you or anyone else who may have overdosed of Atoreza Tablet, please go to the emergency department of the closest hospital or nursing home. Bring a medicine box, container, or label with you to help doctors with necessary information.
* Do not give your medicines to other people even if you know that they have the same condition or it seems that they may have similar conditions. This may lead to overdosage.
* Please consult your physician or pharmacist or product package for more information.

Storage of Atoreza Tablet:
* Store medicines at room temperature, away from heat and direct light. Do not freeze medicines unless required by package insert. Keep medicines away from children and pets.
* Do not flush medications down the toilet or pour them into drainage unless instructed to do so. Medication discarded in this manner may contaminate the environment. Please consult your pharmacist or doctor for more details on how to safely discard Atoreza Tablet.

Expired Atoreza Tablet:
Taking a single dose of expired Atoreza Tablet is unlikely to produce an adverse event. However, please discuss with your primary health provider or pharmacist for proper advice or if you feel unwell or sick. Expired drugs may become ineffective in treating your prescribed conditions. To be on the safe side, it is important not to use expired drugs. If you have a chronic illness that requires taking medicine constantly such as heart condition, seizures, and life-threatening allergies, you are much safer keeping in touch with your primary health care provider so that you can have a fresh supply of unexpired medications.
